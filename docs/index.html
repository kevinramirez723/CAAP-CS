A Brief Introduction to the History of Art


Foreword

Since the beginning of human culture and society, the prevalence of art has been undeniable. From the very earliest of cave paintings, all people regardless of origin share a common interest in the expression of their reality, their emotions, and of their thoughts. As social creatures we tend to share such powerful feeling by way of artistic expression whether it be through writing, dancing, singing, playing an instrument, painting, sculpting, or any vast combination of these and many more. As a result of the extremely enriching and absolutely monumental quantity of created works since the beginning of human history we will sadly only explore a limited few visual forms of expression (with a greater focus on european art) as they change over time. I do however encourage all people to expand their horizons and look towards learning more. Additional resources can be found:



Classical Art

Although there are many significant pieces of art before the Greek and Roman classical period, for the sake of brevity as mentioned in the foreword we will denote this period as our starting point due to its high influence over many later styles and famous artists.



Renaissance Art

Born in Italy, the Renaissance took as its foundation the art of Classical antiquity, but transformed that tradition by absorbing recent developments in the art of Northern Europe and by applying contemporary scientific knowledge.



Baroque and Rococo Art

The Baroque era's claim to fame is the extravegance by which much of the architectual stylings and artistic expression emphasized in combination with the use of dramatic lighting.
